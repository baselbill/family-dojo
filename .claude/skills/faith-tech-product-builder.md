# faith-tech-product-builder Skill

## Purpose

Build faith-integrated products authentically. Helps product teams design apps that serve faith communities with integrity—combining technical product expertise with theological understanding. Handles scripture integration, values-aligned mechanics, sensitive topics (shame, legalism, grace), community marketing, and genuine spiritual value (not just gamification).

## How to Use

Invoke this skill in Claude Code with:
```
/faith-tech-product-builder
```

Then describe your faith-tech idea:
- What app/product? (Bible reader, prayer tracker, values app, devotional platform, etc.)
- Who are users? (individuals, families, churches, youth groups, homeschools)
- What's the core problem you're solving?
- What's your faith tradition/perspective? (Protestant, Catholic, Orthodox, interfaith, etc.)
- Any constraints or values? (no commercialization of scripture, emphasis on grace vs. judgment, etc.)

## What the Skill Does

1. **Theological Assessment** — Is this spiritually sound and authentic?
2. **Product Design** — Mechanics that honor faith, not exploit it
3. **Scripture Integration** — Authentic, not preachy; contextual, not proof-texting
4. **Values Alignment** — Mechanics aligned with your faith tradition
5. **Sensitive Topic Handling** — Grace, shame, legalism, spiritual abuse, burnout
6. **Community Strategy** — Marketing to churches, youth groups, homeschools, faith networks
7. **Advisory Structure** — Theological advisors, community input, accountability
8. **Implementation Roadmap** — MVP, phases, key partnerships, beta testing

## Examples

**Build a prayer habit tracker:**
> "We're building a prayer app for families (kids + parents). Target: make prayer feel like natural rhythm, not checklist. How do we design this so it doesn't become legalistic/shame-based?"

**Launch a Bible reading app:**
> "We want to create a reading plan app with streak tracking, progress milestones, and community features for ages 11-16. How do we integrate scripture authentically without being preachy? What theological perspective should we take?"

**Design a values app:**
> "We're building a values-tracking app for families based on Christian virtues (kindness, courage, honesty, etc.). We want kids to reflect on living out these values, not earn rewards. How should we design this?"

## Core Process

When a user asks to build a faith-tech product, follow this flow:

### 1. VISION CLARIFICATION (2-3 minutes)
Ask clarifying questions to understand:
- What is the core problem you're solving?
- Who are the users? (age, faith tradition, context)
- What's the faith perspective? (Protestant, Catholic, Orthodox, interfaith, other)
- What values are non-negotiable for you?
- What are you worried about? (exploitation, legalism, irrelevance, etc.)
- Who are your advisors or community? (pastors, theologians, parents, etc.)

### 2. THEOLOGICAL ASSESSMENT (5 minutes)
Evaluate spiritual soundness:

**Questions to address**:
- Is this biblically coherent? (Does it honor scripture?)
- Is this theologically responsible? (Does it avoid legalism, shame, prosperity gospel, spiritual abuse patterns?)
- Is this contextually appropriate? (Does it fit the faith tradition you're in?)
- Is this authentic? (Does it ring true, or does it feel exploitative?)
- Is the tone right? (Invitational, not condemning? Graceful, not transactional?)

**Red flags** (address early):
- ⚠️ **Shame-based mechanics**: Penalties for missing days, failure = condemnation
- ⚠️ **Legalism**: Rules without grace, works without faith, performance = worthiness
- ⚠️ **Prosperity gospel**: Wealth/success as sign of faith; poverty as failure
- ⚠️ **Spiritual bypassing**: App replaces actual spiritual direction/community
- ⚠️ **Proof-texting**: Using scripture out of context to support app mechanics
- ⚠️ **False intimacy with scripture**: Treating it like a product to consume
- ⚠️ **Authority claims**: App positioning itself as spiritual authority
- ⚠️ **Exclusivity**: Only "right" way to pray/read/believe

**Strengths to build on**:
- ✅ **Grace-centered**: Emphasizes God's love, not human performance
- ✅ **Community-oriented**: Encourages actual relationships, not just engagement
- ✅ **Honest about struggle**: Acknowledges doubt, questions, spiritual seasons
- ✅ **Humble about limits**: App supports but doesn't replace spiritual practices
- ✅ **Authentic integration**: Scripture feels natural, not forced
- ✅ **Diverse perspectives**: Multiple viewpoints, not claiming final truth

### 3. PRODUCT DESIGN (10 minutes)
Design mechanics that honor faith:

**Core Design Principles for Faith-Tech**:

1. **Grace Over Performance**
   - ❌ Bad: Streaks reset, shame on failure
   - ✅ Good: "Missed a day? That's ok. Come back when ready. God's grace covers."
   - ✅ Good: Grace-period mechanics, comeback narratives, redemption arcs

2. **Invitation Over Obligation**
   - ❌ Bad: "You must pray daily" / daily reminders / notifications
   - ✅ Good: "When you're ready" / optional engagement / user-controlled frequency
   - ✅ Good: Respect for spiritual seasons (rest, fasting, grieving, celebration)

3. **Depth Over Consumption**
   - ❌ Bad: Gamification for gamification's sake (streaks, levels, rewards for reading)
   - ✅ Good: Encourage reflection, journaling, meditation, community discussion
   - ✅ Good: Time for integration, not just checking boxes

4. **Community Over Isolation**
   - ❌ Bad: Solo achievement, leaderboards, comparison
   - ✅ Good: Discussion, prayer circles, sharing, accountability (with consent)
   - ✅ Good: Connection to local church/community, not just digital

5. **Humility Over Authority**
   - ❌ Bad: App as spiritual authority ("this is what God says")
   - ✅ Good: App as companion to spiritual direction, pastor, community
   - ✅ Good: Acknowledges theological diversity, different traditions
   - ✅ Good: Respects user autonomy in interpretation

6. **Authenticity Over Preach**
   - ❌ Bad: Forced faith messaging, inspirational quotes, artificial tone
   - ✅ Good: Natural language, honest tone, room for doubt/questions
   - ✅ Good: Scripture in context, not proof-texting

**Design Components**:

**A. Scripture Integration**
- Provide full passage context, not cherry-picked verses
- Include multiple translations (ESV, NIV, NLT, The Message, etc.)
- Offer commentary from diverse theological sources
- Encourage study (word definitions, historical context, cross-references)
- Allow annotation & journaling
- Never use scripture as reward/punishment mechanism

**B. Mechanics Aligned with Faith**
- **Prayer tracking**: Track frequency, but not as performance metric. Emphasis: "Prayer is conversation, not task."
- **Reading plans**: Support various paces (daily, weekly, seasonal). Grace for missed days. Emphasis: "Scripture is living word."
- **Reflection**: Journaling, meditation tools. Emphasis: "Time to let God speak."
- **Community**: Discussion boards, prayer requests, accountability (optional). Emphasis: "We grow together."
- **Spiritual seasons**: Fast weeks, rest weeks, celebration weeks. Emphasis: "All seasons are sacred."

**C. Sensitive Topic Handling**

**Shame & Failure**:
- No permanent failure states
- No public failure visibility
- Comebacks celebrated, not failures condemned
- Language: "Stepped away? God's grace is new each morning."

**Legalism & Performance**:
- Avoid "best practices" that become rules
- Emphasize that missing doesn't equal unfaithfulness
- Celebrate the why (faith), not just the what (behavior)
- Language: "This is about your relationship with God, not checking boxes."

**Doubt & Questions**:
- Make space for doubt (don't shame it)
- Provide theologians/commentaries with multiple views
- Encourage discussion, not certainty-claiming
- Language: "Doubt is part of faith. It's ok to wrestle."

**Spiritual Abuse & Legalism**:
- Watch for pastor/leader using app to control followers
- Provide resources on spiritual abuse, healthy authority
- Encourage community (not dependency on single leader)
- Language: "Healthy faith communities share power."

**Burnout & Spiritual Seasons**:
- Allow pauses without guilt
- Normalize seasons of fasting, rest, grieving
- Encourage balance (not 24/7 engagement)
- Language: "Rest is spiritual. Sleep is sacred."

### 4. VALUES ALIGNMENT (5 minutes)
Ensure mechanics match faith tradition:

**Protestant considerations**:
- Grace vs. works emphasis
- Scripture centrality
- Denomincational diversity (if applicable)
- Sola fide principles

**Catholic/Orthodox considerations**:
- Role of tradition, sacraments
- Mary & saints (if applicable)
- Liturgical calendar rhythm
- Incarnational theology

**Interfaith considerations**:
- Respect for multiple traditions
- Avoid assumption of universality
- Partner with leaders from each tradition
- Clear about perspective (don't hide it)

**Key questions**:
- Does this honor my faith's core values?
- Would my faith community recognize this as authentic?
- Does this feel like "our app" or "outsider's app"?
- Are there theological trade-offs I'm making? (If yes, are they intentional?)

### 5. COMMUNITY STRATEGY (5 minutes)
Plan authentic engagement with faith communities:

**Advisory Board**:
- Theologian (biblical/theological sound)
- Pastor/leader (community perspective)
- Mental health professional (if handling sensitive topics)
- Parent/youth leader (if targeting families)
- Someone from your target community (lived experience)

**Beta Testing**:
- Don't launch without community input
- Beta test with 20-50 people from target community
- Ask: Does this feel authentic? Exploitative? Helpful? Tone-deaf?
- Iterate based on feedback (not metrics)

**Marketing & Distribution**:
- ❌ Don't: "Christian app for Christians" targeting Christians generically
- ✅ Do: Partner with specific churches, youth groups, homeschool networks
- ✅ Do: Get pastors/leaders to recommend (not pay them, earn recommendation)
- ✅ Do: Build in churches/youth groups, not just app stores
- ✅ Do: Support denominational/tradition-specific needs

**Community Building**:
- Not just user acquisition, but genuine community
- Encourage local groups (prayer circles, study groups, accountability)
- Support leaders & small group facilitators
- Foster discussion, not just consumption

### 6. SENSITIVE TOPIC HANDLING (5 minutes)
Plan for real spiritual struggles:

**Topics to address early**:

**Shame & Condemnation**:
- Users may carry shame from church/upbringing
- App should be grace-centered, not performance-focused
- Offer resources on healthy shame vs. toxic shame
- Language: "You are loved as you are. Nowhere is off-limits."

**Doubt & Deconstruction**:
- Users may be questioning their faith
- Make space for doubt (don't force certainty)
- Offer theology that honors complexity
- Language: "Doubters are welcome here."

**Spiritual Abuse**:
- Users may have experienced manipulation from leaders
- Provide resources on recognizing abuse
- Encourage healthy community, not cultic dependency
- Language: "Healthy faith gives you freedom."

**Grief & Loss**:
- Users may be angry at God, doubting after loss
- Make space for lament (not just praise)
- Offer resources on faith in suffering
- Language: "God is close to the brokenhearted."

**LGBTQ+ Inclusion**:
- If affirming, be explicitly clear
- If traditional, be respectfully clear (and know some will leave)
- Don't pretend neutrality; it's not possible
- Language: "We welcome [explicit statement]"

**Other Issues**:
- Mental health (don't position app as therapy)
- Addiction & recovery (partner with support networks)
- Moral struggles (offer theology, not judgment)
- Wealth & justice (address honestly, not ignored)

**Resources to offer**:
- Theology on the topic (commentaries, articles)
- Professional support (counselors, spiritual directors)
- Community (support groups, prayer circles)
- Honest acknowledgment (church has failed; here's why)

### 7. IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (5 minutes)
Plan phases with theology-first approach:

**Tier 1 (MVP — Faith First)**
- Core scripture integration (clean, contextual)
- Basic engagement feature (prayer tracking, reading plan, reflection)
- Grace-based mechanics (no shame, comeback support)
- Theological review (have advisors vet)
- Advisory board formed
- Beta community identified

**Tier 2 (Community)**
- Community features (discussion, prayer requests, accountability)
- Local group support (small group resources)
- Expanded theological resources (commentaries, multiple views)
- Marketing to churches/youth groups
- First 1,000 users from community (not general launch)

**Tier 3 (Depth)**
- Advanced features (custom plans, integration with liturgy, seasonal content)
- Broader denomination/tradition support
- Professional partnerships (counseling, spiritual direction)
- Sustainability model (ethical monetization, not exploitative)

**Not On Roadmap** (protect against):
- ❌ Aggressive growth metrics (prioritize authenticity)
- ❌ Premium/paywall on core spiritual content
- ❌ Data mining faith behavior for ad targeting
- ❌ Celebrity pastor partnerships (unless authentic)
- ❌ Prosperity gospel expansion
- ❌ One-size-fits-all theology

### 8. METRICS THAT MATTER (3 minutes)
Track the right things:

**What NOT to measure**:
- ❌ DAU/MAU (encourages addiction, not spirituality)
- ❌ Streak length (encourages legalism)
- ❌ Engagement time (rewards busyness, not depth)
- ❌ Growth rate (may indicate hype, not authenticity)

**What TO measure**:
- ✅ Community health (activity in discussion, prayer requests, encouragement)
- ✅ Retention of meaningful users (people who say "this changed my faith")
- ✅ Theological feedback (advisors say "this is sound")
- ✅ User testimonials (not just ratings, but stories)
- ✅ Church partnerships (how many congregations use this?)
- ✅ Absence of harm (are people experiencing shame? legalism? burnout?)
- ✅ Advisor satisfaction (are theological leaders comfortable endorsing?)

**How to measure**:
- Quarterly surveys with open questions
- Community feedback (what are people saying in forums?)
- Advisory board check-ins
- User interviews (not data harvesting)
- Theological review of user feedback

## Key Principles

**Be Theologically Humble**
- You're not the authority on faith
- Get advisors, listen to community
- Acknowledge you might be wrong
- Respect different traditions

**Protect Against Exploitation**
- Faith is vulnerable; don't exploit it
- Watch for shame-based mechanics creeping in
- Be honest about monetization (don't hide it)
- Don't sell what should be free (scripture, prayer, relationship)

**Build With Community, Not For It**
- Don't make decisions alone
- Beta test with real users
- Get advisor feedback (not rubber-stamp)
- Iterate based on theology & community feedback

**Authenticity > Growth**
- Slow growth with real community > viral growth with fake enthusiasm
- Better to serve 10,000 well than reach 1 million poorly
- Quality of community matters more than quantity

**Honor Spiritual Realities**
- Faith is real, not just engagement metric
- Spiritual growth is slow, not linear
- Doubt is part of faith, not failure
- Grace is central, not peripheral
